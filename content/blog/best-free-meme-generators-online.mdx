## What makes a good meme generator

Three things matter, regardless of the tool:

1. **The classic templates are there.** Drake, Distracted Boyfriend, Woman Yelling at Cat, This Is Fine. If a meme generator can&apos;t produce these in two clicks, you&apos;ll never reach for it.
2. **The classic meme font is the default.** Impact, bold, white fill, black outline, ALL CAPS. Every meme generator that nails this looks right out of the box. Every one that doesn&apos;t feels off, and most people can&apos;t articulate why.
3. **Custom uploads work.** Sometimes you need a meme template that hasn&apos;t made it into the canon yet, or the joke only works with your own photo as the background. Tools that lock you into their template library are useless half the time.

Almost everything else — text positioning, font choice, advanced overlays — is a bonus.

## The default meme look

Every tool worth using produces the same look out of the box: **Impact font, white fill, black stroke, ALL CAPS, anchored at the top and bottom**. This is the look that defined image macros since the late 2000s. Anything else feels wrong, even if you can&apos;t say why.

If a tool defaults to anything other than this — sans-serif, no outline, sentence case — change the settings or pick a different tool. The classic look is the classic look for a reason: it&apos;s instantly readable on any background and unmistakably "this is a meme".

<Callout type="tip">
**The double-pass stroke matters.** A good meme generator renders the outline (`strokeText`) *first*, then the white fill (`fillText`) on top. Done in that order, the outline forms a clean halo around each letter. Tools that do it the other way around produce a weirdly pixelated outline that subtly looks wrong.
</Callout>

## Frequently asked

### Will my meme have a watermark?

Depends on the tool. UtilityApps and Make a Meme don&apos;t watermark free output. Imgflip and Kapwing add a small site URL to the bottom of every free meme. For posts you don&apos;t want branded, pick one of the watermark-free tools.

### Can I use these tools commercially?

Read each tool&apos;s terms of service. UtilityApps imposes no commercial restrictions on output. Imgflip and Kapwing have specific clauses about reselling templates or using their site brand in monetised work. Custom-uploaded photos are always your own to use.

### What file format should I save the meme as?

PNG, always. Memes are simple visuals with text — they&apos;re small files in PNG and the lossless format keeps the text crisp. JPEG at lower quality can introduce visible compression artefacts around the letters that ruin the classic meme look.

### Can I make GIF memes?

For animated memes, build them from a sequence of JPGs in the [animated GIF maker](/tools/convert-from-jpg) and then download the .gif file. UtilityApps&apos; meme generator itself outputs PNG only — it&apos;s for still memes.

### What about adding extra captions, not just top and bottom text?

The classic meme has just top and bottom text. For multi-panel memes (Drake, Distracted Boyfriend, Expanding Brain) and labelled diagrams, you need advanced positioning. UtilityApps supports custom overlays with drag-to-position handles; Imgflip and Kapwing have similar advanced modes.
