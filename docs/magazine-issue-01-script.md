Segment timings are suggestions for a ~12-minute video — trim VO if you need it tighter.

## SEGMENT 1 — WHY UTILITYAPPS SHOULD BE THE TAB YOU OPEN FIRST (0:45–2:15)

**VISUAL:** Split-screen — left side shows someone juggling five different tool sites (upload walls, cookie banners, "create a free account" prompts); right side shows utilityapps.site/tools with one search bar and instant results.

**VO:**
> Here's the pitch, stripped down: most of the internet's utility tools were built to capture your email before they'll do anything useful. Ours weren't.
>
> UtilityApps is a single home for the tools you reach for during an actual workday — merge a PDF, compress a video, generate an invoice, write a privacy policy, translate a paragraph, build a QR code. A hundred and ten of them, right now, across eighteen categories, and every one of them opens in seconds.
>
> Most run entirely in your browser. That's not a marketing line — it's an architecture decision. Your PDF doesn't get uploaded to a server to be merged. Your video doesn't leave your device to be trimmed. The processing happens on your machine, using the same engines browsers already ship with. Which means: nothing to wait on, nothing sitting on someone else's disk, and nothing you have to trust us with in the first place.
>
> That's the case for why this becomes the tab you open first at work. Not because it's one more tool — because it quietly replaces the twelve tabs you had bookmarked for this exact purpose.

**ON-SCREEN (stat card):**
```
110+ tools · 18 categories · 0 signups required · 100% free
```

## SEGMENT 3 — INSIDE THE TOOLBOX: A QUICK TOUR (3:30–5:15)

**VISUAL:** Fast montage, one tool per category, 3–4 seconds each, screen-recorded doing the actual task.

**VO:**
> Before the update log, a quick tour — because "110 tools" doesn't mean anything until you see the range.
>
> **PDF Tools** — merge, split, compress, rotate, watermark, sign, and convert to and from Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Ten of them run entirely client-side; six heavier conversions are handled through a conversion engine when the browser genuinely can't do it alone.
>
> **Image and Video Tools** — compress, convert, crop, resize, remove backgrounds, extract audio, trim clips — video processing runs through ffmpeg compiled to run inside your browser. No upload, no rendering queue.
>
> **Legal Tools** — eight document generators: privacy policies, terms of service, NDAs, freelance contracts, DMCA takedowns, GDPR requests, cease-and-desist letters. Every one of them builds the document client-side, so the sensitive details you type — names, addresses, terms — never touch our servers.
>
> **Business, Finance, Student, Developer, Design, Travel, Sleep, Health, Productivity, SEO, Text, and Language Tools** round out the library — everything from a JWT decoder to a nap calculator to an on-device translator that never sends your text anywhere.
>
> And the newest addition: **Share** — paste a text snippet, shorten a URL, or drop a file up to 25 megabytes, and get a link back. No account. Nothing kept longer than you want it kept.

## SEGMENT 4 — SINCE LAUNCH: EVERYTHING THAT SHIPPED (5:15–8:00)

**VISUAL:** Scrolling changelog timeline, styled like the site's actual `/changelog` page, dates highlighted as VO calls them out.

**VO:**
> This is the section returning readers should care about most — because if you used UtilityApps back in May and haven't checked in since, a lot has changed.
>
> **May 23rd** — we launched two categories in one day: **PDF Tools**, with ten in-browser tools plus six Office-to-PDF conversions, and **Legal Tools**, with all eight document generators live at once.
>
> **May 26th** — **Share** launched. Phase one shipped text snippets and a no-signup URL shortener. It was popular enough, fast enough, that we didn't wait long for phase two.
>
> **May 27th** — two things landed the same day. First, **error tracking and completion analytics** went live behind the scenes — not a feature you'll see, but it means when something breaks, we know within minutes instead of finding out from an angry email. Second, we opened the **API waitlist** — more on that in a minute.
>
> **May 28th** — **Language Tools** launched, headlined by a **Private Translator** that runs the translation model on your device. Your text never reaches a server. Same day, we shipped a **"Translate this page"** control that appears site-wide, so the whole platform — not just one tool — is reachable in more languages.
>
> **May 29th** — we opened the **Chrome extension waitlist**. Also more on that shortly.
>
> **June 1st** — the biggest single-day ship of the quarter. **Share Phase 2** added file uploads up to 25 megabytes, going straight to secure storage instead of through our own servers — which matters because it means large files don't hit the size limits our hosting would otherwise impose. Same day, we published **44 head-to-head comparison pages and 14 alternative-to pages** — real, fact-checked comparisons against tools like iLovePDF, Smallpdf, Adobe, TinyPNG, remove.bg, and Photopea, each one dated so you know it's current. And we added **star ratings** across every tool page — visible, and only shown once a tool has enough real ratings to mean something.
>
> Two months. Two full categories, a share tool built in two phases, an on-device translator, sitewide translation, and the analytics groundwork to keep improving faster from here. That's the pace we're aiming to keep.

## SEGMENT 6 — BEHIND THE CURTAIN: THE ADSENSE CLIMB (9:00–9:50)

**VISUAL:** A more candid, slower tone here — maybe a static shot of the review dashboard blurred out, or just a plain title card. This is the "honest moment" of the magazine.

**VO:**
> In the spirit of actually being transparent in a magazine that claims to be data-driven — here's a real obstacle. Getting a new site approved for **Google AdSense** has been one of the harder parts of this launch.
>
> It's not one clean pass-or-fail. Google wants sustained original content, real traffic patterns, clear navigation, and policy compliance across every single page — and a site with over a hundred tools and dozens of legal and comparison pages means over a hundred opportunities to get flagged for something minor. We've gone through review cycles, made changes, and gone through review again.
>
> We're saying this out loud for two reasons. One — if you run a site and you're stuck in the same review loop, you're not doing anything unusually wrong; it's just a genuinely slow, opaque process. Two — it's exactly why we're opening direct advertising now instead of waiting on approval to fund what we build next.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Tone:** Confident but candid — this issue works because Segment 6 (AdSense) and Segment 8 (the ask) are honest rather than purely promotional. Don't cut those for time before cutting elsewhere.
- **Screen recordings needed:** live captures of Merge PDF, Compress Video, Private Translator, Share (file upload), and the `/changelog` page scrolling — these carry Segments 3–4 and are worth shooting in 1080p/60fps for crisp zooms.
- **Data disclosure:** the Segment 2 numbers are from a recent traffic snapshot, not lifetime totals since May — the VO is phrased to reflect that ("a recent snapshot") rather than implying a cumulative total. Keep that phrasing if you update the numbers closer to publish date.
- **CTAs to keep visible in the YouTube description:** advertising contact, API waitlist link, Chrome extension waitlist link, contact/feedback link.
