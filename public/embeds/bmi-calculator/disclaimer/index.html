Limitations of BMI and Other Calculators

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure. Known limitations include:

  • BMI does not directly measure body fat or lean muscle mass
  • Athletes and muscular individuals may have a high BMI despite low body fat
  • BMI may underestimate health risks in older adults with lower muscle mass
  • BMI cutoffs were derived from populations of European descent and may not reflect risk equally across all ethnic groups
  • BMI does not account for fat distribution (e.g., abdominal vs. peripheral fat)

The calorie, body fat, ideal weight, waist-hip ratio, and children's BMI calculators all provide population-level estimates only and must not be used for clinical decision-making without professional medical oversight.
