How a HELOC Works

A Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) lets you borrow against your home equity up to a set credit limit. It works like a credit card — you draw funds as needed, repay them, and draw again during the draw period.

Draw Period (typically 5–10 years): You can borrow up to your credit limit and usually make interest-only payments on the outstanding balance.

Repayment Period (typically 10–20 years): No further draws. You repay the outstanding balance through fully amortized principal + interest payments.

HELOCs typically have variable interest rates tied to the prime rate. Your lender generally allows borrowing up to 80–85% of your home's value, minus your existing mortgage balance.
