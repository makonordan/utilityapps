Black Friday, end of year sales, clearance pricing — retailers stack discounts and use psychological pricing to make deals look bigger than they are. Enter the original price and discount before you buy so you always know exactly what you're paying and saving.

How to Calculate a Discount

Whether you're at a checkout, comparing deals online, or working out how much you'll save in a sale, discount calculations follow one straightforward formula. Understanding it lets you verify any advertised price in seconds.

The Formula

Savings = Original Price × (Discount% ÷ 100)

Final Price = Original Price − Savings

— or combined —

Final Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100)

The expression (1 − Discount ÷ 100) is called the price multiplier. For a 25% discount the multiplier is 0.75, meaning you pay 75¢ for every dollar of the original price.

Step-by-Step Example: 30% off $120

  1. Convert 30% to a decimal: 30 ÷ 100 = 0.30
  2. Calculate savings: $120 × 0.30 = $36
  3. Subtract from original: $120 − $36 = $84
  4. Answer: final price is $84, you save $36

Mental Math Shortcuts for Common Discounts

  • 10% off — move the decimal one place left, then subtract (10% of $85 = $8.50, final = $76.50)
  • 20% off — find 10%, double it, subtract (10% of $60 = $6 → savings $12 → final $48)
  • 25% off — divide by 4 to get savings ($100 ÷ 4 = $25 off → final $75)
  • 50% off — divide by 2 (final price is exactly half)
  • 15% off — find 10% and 5% (half of 10%), add them ($80: 10% = $8, 5% = $4, total $12 off → $68)

Stacked Discounts — A Common Trap

When two discounts are applied in sequence, the second discount applies to the already-reduced price, not the original. A 20% off coupon on top of a 10% sale is not 30% off.

  • Original price: $100
  • After 10% sale: $100 × 0.90 = $90
  • After 20% coupon: $90 × 0.80 = $72
  • Combined effect: 28% off — not 30%

To find the combined multiplier for any stack of discounts, multiply the individual multipliers together: 0.90 × 0.80 = 0.72, meaning you pay 72% of the original.

Working Backwards: Find the Original Price

If you know the sale price and the discount but want the original price, divide the sale price by (1 − Discount ÷ 100). For example, a $75 item after 25% off: $75 ÷ 0.75 = $100 original. This is useful when a tag shows only the final price.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a discount?▶
Multiply the original price by the discount percentage divided by 100 to get the savings amount, then subtract from the original price. Formula: Final Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). For example, 25% off $80: $80 × 0.75 = $60.
What is 20% off $50?▶
20% off $50 is $40. Savings = $50 × 0.20 = $10. Final price = $50 − $10 = $40. You can also multiply directly: $50 × 0.80 = $40.
What is 30% off $100?▶
30% off $100 is $70. Savings = $100 × 0.30 = $30. Final price = $100 − $30 = $70. Quick check: 30% of 100 is always 30, regardless of currency.
What is the formula for calculating a sale price?▶
Sale Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount ÷ 100). The expression (1 − Discount ÷ 100) is called the multiplier. For a 25% discount the multiplier is 0.75, meaning you pay 75% of the original price.
What is 15% off $80?▶
$80 with 15% off costs $68. Savings = $80 × 0.15 = $12. Final price = $80 − $12 = $68. Alternatively: $80 × 0.85 = $68.
How do stacked or double discounts work?▶
Stacked discounts are applied sequentially, not added together. A 20% discount followed by a further 10% off is NOT 30% off. It is: apply 20% first ($100 → $80), then 10% of that ($80 × 0.90 = $72). The combined effect is 28% off, not 30%.
Can this calculator handle any currency?▶
Yes — the calculator supports USD, EUR, GBP, NGN (Nigerian Naira), GHS (Ghanaian Cedi), KES (Kenyan Shilling), and ZAR (South African Rand). The currency selector changes the symbol; the underlying calculation is the same for all currencies.
What is 50% off — is it the same as half price?▶
Yes. 50% off means you pay exactly half the original price. 50% off $120 = $60. This is the easiest discount to calculate mentally: simply divide the original price by 2.
