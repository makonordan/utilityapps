Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my grade percentage?▶
Divide your score by the total possible points and multiply by 100. For example, if you scored 76 out of 90: (76 ÷ 90) × 100 = 84.4%. That rounds to a B letter grade in the US system.
How is GPA calculated?▶
Each letter grade is converted to a grade point (A = 4.0, B = 3.0, etc.). Each grade point is then multiplied by the course credit hours to get quality points. GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Hours. For example, a 3-credit A (4.0) and a 3-credit B (3.0) gives (12 + 9) ÷ 6 = 3.5 GPA.
What is a good GPA?▶
In the US 4.0 scale: 3.5–4.0 is typically Dean's List or Honors level; 3.0–3.4 is considered good; 2.5–2.9 is satisfactory; below 2.0 may put you on academic probation. Graduate school admission often requires a 3.0 minimum. Many competitive programs and employers look for 3.5+.
What grade do I need on my final exam?▶
Use the Grade Needed tab. Enter your current coursework grade, the weight of the final exam (e.g. 40%), and your target final grade. The formula is: Needed Score = (Target − Current × (1 − Exam Weight ÷ 100)) ÷ (Exam Weight ÷ 100). If the result exceeds 100%, you cannot reach the target even with a perfect final exam score.
What do WAEC grades A1–F9 mean?▶
The West African Examinations Council (WAEC) and NECO use a 9-point scale. A1 (75–100%) and B2–B3 (65–74%) are distinction/very good grades. C4–C6 (50–64%) are Credit grades required for university admission in most Nigerian courses. D7–E8 (40–49%) are passes. F9 (0–39%) is a fail. Most Nigerian universities require five Credits including English and Mathematics for admission.
How does the UK grading system work?▶
UK universities award degree classifications rather than letter grades. A First Class degree requires 70%+. An Upper Second (2:1) is 60–69% and is the standard requirement for graduate jobs and postgraduate courses. A Lower Second (2:2) is 50–59%. A Third is 40–49%. Below 40% typically results in an Ordinary (unclassified) degree rather than an Honours degree.
What is the difference between weighted and unweighted GPA?▶
An unweighted GPA treats every course equally on a 0–4.0 scale. A weighted GPA gives bonus points for harder courses — AP or IB classes often count as 5.0 for an A, honors as 4.5. Weighted GPAs can exceed 4.0. Most US college admissions offices recalculate GPA on an unweighted basis for comparison, but a strong weighted GPA signals academic ambition.
How do I convert a percentage to a letter grade in the US system?▶
90–100% = A, 80–89% = B, 70–79% = C, 60–69% = D, below 60% = F. Many schools use plus/minus grades: 93–100 = A, 90–92 = A−, 87–89 = B+, 83–86 = B, 80–82 = B−, and so on in 3-point bands. Always check your institution's specific grading policy as cutoffs vary.
