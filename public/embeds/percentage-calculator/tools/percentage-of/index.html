How to Calculate a Percentage of a Number

Finding a percentage of a number is one of the most common calculations in everyday life — from working out a restaurant tip to understanding a sale discount. The good news: it always follows the same simple formula.

The Formula

Result = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Number

Dividing by 100 converts the percentage into a decimal (its true mathematical form), and multiplying by the number gives you the portion represented by that percentage.

Step-by-Step Example: What is 15% of 200?

  1. Convert 15% to a decimal: 15 ÷ 100 = 0.15
  2. Multiply by 200: 0.15 × 200 = 30
  3. Answer: 15% of 200 = 30

Mental Math Shortcuts for Common Percentages

For quick estimates without a calculator, these shortcuts are invaluable:

  • 1% — move the decimal two places left (1% of 350 = 3.5)
  • 10% — move the decimal one place left (10% of 250 = 25)
  • 5% — find 10%, then halve it (5% of 250 = 12.5)
  • 20% — find 10%, then double it (20% of 250 = 50)
  • 25% — divide by 4 (25% of 200 = 50)
  • 50% — divide by 2 (50% of 200 = 100)
  • 75% — find 50% + 25% (75% of 200 = 100 + 50 = 150)

Real-World Uses

  • Shopping discounts— “25% off a $120 jacket” means you save 0.25 × 120 = $30, paying only $90.
  • Restaurant tips — an 18% tip on a $65 bill is 0.18 × 65 = $11.70.
  • Sales tax — 8.5% tax on a $200 purchase adds 0.085 × 200 = $17 to your total.
  • Exam scores — scoring 87% on a 120-point exam means 0.87 × 120 = 104.4 points needed to achieve that grade.
  • Investment returns — a 7% annual return on $10,000 earns 0.07 × 10,000 = $700 in the first year.
