Ratios, Fractions, and Percentages

A ratio describes a proportional relationship between two quantities. The ratio 3:4 says “3 parts for every 4 total parts.” A percentage says the same thing on a scale of 100. Both representations are equivalent — they are different languages for the same idea.

Part-to-Whole vs Part-to-Part Ratios

There are two kinds of ratios, and confusing them is a common source of errors. A part-to-whole ratio (3:4) compares a subset to the entire group. This converts directly to a percentage: 3 ÷ 4 × 100 = 75%. A part-to-part ratio (3:1) compares two subsets to each other. To convert it, first calculate the total: 3 + 1 = 4, then 3 ÷ 4 = 75% for the first part. This calculator uses part-to-whole ratios.

Simplifying Ratios with the GCD

Any ratio can be simplified by dividing both numbers by their Greatest Common Divisor. For example, 75:100 — the GCD of 75 and 100 is 25. Dividing both by 25 gives 3:4. The simplified ratio is mathematically identical to the original; it is simply easier to read and communicate.

Real-World Uses

  • Maps: a scale of 1:50,000 means 1 cm on the map = 50,000 cm in reality
  • Recipes: a 2:1 flour-to-butter ratio helps scale quantities up or down
  • Finance: a 60:40 equity-to-bond portfolio split equals 60% stocks
  • Exams: passing 36 out of 45 questions is a 4:5 ratio = 80%

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert a ratio to a percentage?▶
Divide the part by the total (denominator), then multiply by 100. For example, the ratio 3:4 means 3 out of every 4 parts, so (3 ÷ 4) × 100 = 75%. The formula is: Percentage = (Part ÷ Total) × 100.
How do I convert a percentage to a ratio?▶
Express the percentage as a fraction over 100, then simplify using the GCD. For example, 75% = 75/100. GCD(75, 100) = 25, so divide both by 25 to get 3/4, meaning the simplified ratio is 3:4.
What is the ratio equivalent of 75%?▶
75% = 75/100. Dividing both by GCD(75, 100) = 25 gives 3/4, so the ratio is 3:4. This means 3 parts out of every 4 total parts, or equivalently, 3 parts for every 1 non-part (3:1 in part-to-part notation).
What is the difference between a part-to-part and part-to-whole ratio?▶
A part-to-whole ratio (like 3:4) compares a part to the entire group — 3 out of 4 total. This converts directly to a percentage. A part-to-part ratio (like 3:1) compares two parts to each other — 3 of one thing for every 1 of another. To convert a part-to-part ratio to a percentage, first find the total: 3 + 1 = 4, then 3/4 = 75%. This calculator uses part-to-whole notation.
How do I simplify a ratio?▶
Find the GCD (Greatest Common Divisor) of both numbers, then divide each by it. For example, to simplify 12:16, GCD(12, 16) = 4, so 12 ÷ 4 = 3 and 16 ÷ 4 = 4, giving 3:4. To find the GCD manually, list the factors of each number and identify the largest common one.
Is a ratio the same as a fraction?▶
A ratio is closely related to a fraction. A ratio A:B can be written as the fraction A/B. However, a ratio compares two quantities while a fraction represents a part of a whole. When the denominator of the fraction equals the total (A + B or just B in part-to-whole notation), the fraction directly converts to a percentage by multiplying by 100.
