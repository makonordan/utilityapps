ℹ️Tax-Inclusive vs. Tax-Exclusive Prices

Tax-exclusive (pre-tax, ex-VAT): the displayed price does not include tax — tax is added on top at checkout. Common in B2B invoicing and US retail shelf prices. Use Add Tax mode.

Tax-inclusive (inc. VAT, GST-inclusive): the displayed price already contains the tax. Standard in UK, Australia, and most of Europe. Use Remove Tax mode to find the pre-tax amount.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a tax-inclusive and tax-exclusive price?▶
A tax-exclusive price (also called pre-tax or ex-VAT) does not include tax — tax is added on top at checkout. A tax-inclusive price already contains the tax within the displayed amount. In the UK and Australia, prices are usually shown tax-inclusive. In the US, shelf prices are typically tax-exclusive.
How do I calculate the price with VAT?▶
Multiply the pre-tax price by (1 + VAT rate ÷ 100). For example, a £50 item at 20% VAT: £50 × 1.20 = £60 total. The tax amount alone is £50 × 0.20 = £10.
How do I remove VAT from a price?▶
Divide the VAT-inclusive price by (1 + VAT rate ÷ 100). For example, a £120 total at 20% VAT: £120 ÷ 1.20 = £100 pre-tax price. The VAT amount is £120 − £100 = £20. This is the "Remove Tax" mode in the calculator above.
What is the formula for reverse tax (removing tax)?▶
Pre-tax price = Tax-inclusive price ÷ (1 + Tax Rate ÷ 100). For a 15% tax on a $115 total: $115 ÷ 1.15 = $100 pre-tax. A common mistake is to simply multiply by (1 − rate), which gives the wrong answer.
What is the UK VAT rate?▶
The UK standard VAT rate is 20%. A reduced rate of 5% applies to domestic energy (electricity, gas) and children's car seats. Most food, children's clothing, books, and medicines are zero-rated (0% VAT).
What is the VAT rate in Nigeria?▶
Nigeria's VAT rate is 7.5%, raised from 5% in February 2020 under the Finance Act. Basic food, agricultural products, pharmaceutical products, and educational materials are exempt from VAT.
How does US sales tax work?▶
The United States has no federal sales tax. Each state sets its own rate, ranging from 0% in states like Oregon, Montana, and New Hampshire to around 9.55% in Tennessee. Counties and cities can add local taxes on top, making the effective rate as high as 12–13% in some areas.
What is the difference between VAT, GST, and sales tax?▶
VAT (Value-Added Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Tax) are collected at each stage of production and distribution; the end consumer bears the final cost. They are broadly equivalent — GST is the term used in Australia, Canada, India, and New Zealand. Sales tax (used in the USA) is collected only at the final point of sale and is not embedded in the production chain.
