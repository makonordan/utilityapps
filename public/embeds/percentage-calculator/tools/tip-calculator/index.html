Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I tip at a restaurant?▶
In the US and Canada, 15–20% is standard for a full-service restaurant. 15% signals satisfactory service, 18–20% is good to great, and 25%+ is exceptional. In the UK and Australia, 10–15% is common, though tipping is more optional.
How do I calculate a 20% tip quickly?▶
Find 10% of the bill (move the decimal one place left), then double it. For a $65 bill: 10% = $6.50, doubled = $13.00. That's your 20% tip, for a total of $78.00.
Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax total?▶
Either is acceptable. Tipping on the pre-tax amount is technically more accurate — you're rewarding service, not the government's tax. However, most people tip on the post-tax total shown on the bill for simplicity. The difference is usually under $1.
How do I split a restaurant bill equally?▶
Use the split counter in the calculator above. Enter the bill amount and tip percentage, then increase the split count to the number of diners. The calculator shows exactly what each person owes, including their share of the tip.
Is tipping mandatory?▶
In most countries, tipping is voluntary. In the US and Canada, however, not tipping at a full-service restaurant is considered rude — servers rely on tips as a significant part of their income. Always check your bill first, as some restaurants automatically add a service charge for large parties.
What is a good tip for food delivery?▶
10–20% is standard for food delivery, with a minimum of $3–5 for small orders. Tip on the higher end for long-distance deliveries, bad weather, large or complex orders, and great service. Delivery drivers often earn below minimum wage before tips.
